CPU (Central Processing Unit.)

A central processing unit (CPU), also called a central processor, main processor, or just processor, is the most important processor in a given computer. Its electronic circuitry executes instructions of a computer program, such as arithmetic, logic, controlling, and input/output operations.

In processor options for PC, there are only 2 options (excepting apple processors and others.) Intel or AMD, both company's make good CPU's "However depending on which you choose Intel or AMD you will need to buy a specific mother board that is compatible with your processor".

Note: There are some CPUs that have internal graphics and do not require a dedicated GPU to process the graphics or images on your computer "Remember to review if your CPU has or not internal graphics since if not, and you do not buy a GPU your PC will not display any image.", however having a dedicated graphic card can make much powerfully your computer which you may need if you would like to run heavy resolutions, Programs or some video games. as well CPU's that don't have internal graphics are more powerfull since all of its cores will be dedicated only in processing programs and not images or videos.

RAM (Random access memory)

Random-access memory (RAM) is a form of electronic computer memory that can be read and changed in any order, typically used to store working data and machine code. A random-access memory device allows data items to be read or written in almost the same amount of time irrespective of the physical location of data inside the memory, in contrast with other direct-access data storage media.

The RAM capacity and speed may be an important factor if you would like to have multiple programs, web pages, ETC. Running at the same time, not having enough space may cause freezing images, sudden close of programs running and a bad experience when using it.

Storage Memory

Files and data are saved on a storage drive, either a hard disk drive (HDD) or a solid state drive (SSD). Although HDDs have traditionally offered greater storage capacity which gives a good benefit for their cost, SSDs have made them practically obsolete; They run 6 times faster on average and are 90 times more energy efficient than HDDs.

Note: there are also M.2 An M.2 SSD is a small form factor solid-state drive (SSD) that internally mounted storage expansion cards use. M.2 SSDs conform to a computer industry specification and are designed to enable high-performance storage in thin, power-constrained devices, such as Ultrabook laptops and tablet computers.

Power Supply

A power supply unit (PSU) converts mains AC to low-voltage regulated DC power for the internal components of a computer. Modern personal computers universally use switched-mode power supplies. Some power supplies have a manual switch for selecting input voltage, while others automatically adapt to the main voltage.

Note: the power suppliers have different amount of watts it can supply. Check your components to see how much of watts they may use to be sure the power supply you buy provides enough watts, so the PC is able to turn on (Tip: if you are buying high or elite performance components don't buy a low or medium performance power supply since it may not provide enough watts to turn on.). It is recommended to buy a power supply that can supply more watts than what your PC needs since if not needed the power supply will not provide all its power, and you have extra capacity in case you would like to make future upgrades to your PC and a good power supply protects your PC from electric issues that can destroy your PC.
